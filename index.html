Ok, phew, everything is in order now. But, how many commits did that turn into? Type git log origin.. to look at all the commits you haven't pushed yet. Three of them for this change? That's a bit much. Let's do something called a squash or fixup. git rebase -i opens the interactive rebase. Here we can do a bunch of things to our commits, as described. Feel free to investigate the other options on your own, but we will discuss squash and fixup. They both do basically the same thing, merging two or more commits into one. When you write squash or fixup by a commit, it will be merged into the closest commit with pick above it. The difference between them is Squash keeps the commit messages and displays all squashed commit messages along with the picked one, letting you choose what to keep.
